# Dispute Handling Policy

## Policy ID: POL-DISP-001
## Version: 2024-01
## Intent: DISPUTE
## Last Updated: January 10, 2024

## Overview

This policy establishes procedures for handling customer disputes regarding unauthorized transactions, billing errors, and merchant-related issues. We are committed to fair, timely resolution while protecting customers from fraudulent charges.

## Types of Disputes

### 1. Unauthorized Transactions
- Charges made without customer's knowledge or consent
- Stolen card usage
- Account takeover scenarios
- Identity theft-related charges

### 2. Billing Errors
- Duplicate charges
- Incorrect charge amount
- Charges for cancelled services/subscriptions
- Pre-authorization holds not released
- Currency conversion errors

### 3. Merchant Disputes
- Goods not received
- Goods significantly different from description
- Services not rendered as agreed
- Quality issues with products/services
- Merchant refusal to honor return policy

### 4. Technical Errors
- ATM dispensing errors
- Point-of-sale system malfunctions
- Mobile payment processing issues
- Online banking transfer errors

## Customer Rights

### Federal Protections

**Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA) - Credit Cards:**
- $50 maximum liability for unauthorized charges
- Zero liability if reported before card is used
- 60 days to dispute billing errors from statement date
- Provisional credit during investigation

**Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA) - Debit Cards:**
- $50 liability if reported within 2 business days
- $500 liability if reported within 60 days
- Unlimited liability after 60 days
- 10 business days for provisional credit

### Bank Zero Liability Policy
We exceed federal requirements:
- **$0 liability** for unauthorized transactions when reported promptly
- 24/7 dispute reporting
- Immediate card deactivation
- Expedited replacement card (1-3 business days)

## Filing a Dispute

### Step 1: Initial Report
**Online/Mobile App:**
- Navigate to transaction history
- Select disputed transaction
- Choose dispute reason from dropdown
- Provide details in text field
- Upload supporting documentation
- Submit electronically

**Phone:**
- Call customer service: 1-800-555-BANK (2265)
- Available 24/7 for fraud/unauthorized transactions
- Business hours for merchant disputes
- Reference number provided immediately

**In-Branch:**
- Visit any branch location
- Bring transaction documentation
- Complete dispute form with banker assistance
- Receive case number and estimated timeline

### Step 2: Information Required
**Basic Information:**
- Account number and transaction date
- Transaction amount and merchant name
- Description of the issue
- When you first noticed the issue

**Supporting Documentation (if applicable):**
- Receipts or order confirmations
- Communication with merchant
- Proof of cancellation
- Shipping/tracking information
- Police report (for theft/fraud)
- Photos of defective merchandise

### Step 3: Provisional Credit
**Eligible Transactions:**
- Unauthorized charges (immediate)
- Billing errors over $50 (within 10 days)
- Merchant disputes over $100 (within 10 days)

**Credit Amount:**
- Full disputed amount
- Associated fees (overdraft, late fees)
- Currency conversion charges (if applicable)

**Conditions:**
- Subject to reversal if dispute not upheld
- Must cooperate with investigation
- Cannot dispute same transaction twice

## Investigation Process

### Phase 1: Initial Review (1-3 business days)
- Verify transaction details in system
- Check for duplicate disputes
- Assess fraud indicators
- Determine dispute category
- Assign to appropriate team

### Phase 2: Evidence Gathering (5-10 business days)
- Contact merchant for transaction details
- Review card network documentation
- Analyze transaction patterns
- Interview customer if needed
- Consult fraud prevention team

### Phase 3: Analysis (3-5 business days)
- Compare customer claim with merchant evidence
- Apply card network rules
- Evaluate supporting documentation
- Consider account history
- Determine liability

### Phase 4: Resolution (1-2 business days)
- Make final determination
- Notify customer of outcome
- Issue permanent credit or reverse provisional credit
- Update account notes
- Close case

## Merchant Chargeback Process

### When We Initiate Chargeback
- Customer evidence supports claim
- Merchant unresponsive to inquiry
- Transaction violates card network rules
- Clear unauthorized usage
- Merchant failed to deliver goods/services

### Chargeback Reason Codes
- **10.4**: Card absent fraud
- **13.1**: Merchandise/services not received
- **13.2**: Cancelled recurring transaction
- **13.3**: Not as described or defective
- **13.5**: Misrepresentation
- **30**: Services not provided or merchandise defective

### Merchant Response
- Merchant has 30 days to respond with evidence
- May provide proof of delivery, signature, terms of service
- We review merchant's rebuttal evidence
- Customer notified of merchant response
- May escalate to card network arbitration

### Arbitration
- If merchant contests our chargeback
- Card network makes final decision
- Binding on both parties
- Rare for routine disputes
- Customer notified of arbitration outcome

## Special Situations

### Recurring Transactions
- Customer must attempt cancellation with merchant first
- Provide proof of cancellation attempt
- We can block future charges from merchant
- Past charges may still be disputed normally
- Consider closing card and issuing new number

### Digital Wallet Disputes
- Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay
- Same process as physical card disputes
- May need device information
- Biometric/PIN usage considered
- Token-specific investigation

### International Transactions
- Currency conversion may complicate amounts
- Time zone differences in transaction timing
- Language barriers with international merchants
- Extended investigation timeline (up to 120 days)
- May require additional documentation

### Family Member/Authorized User Disputes
- Transactions by authorized users typically not disputable
- Must remove authorization before disputing
- Consider as unauthorized only if:
  - Authorization was revoked before transaction
  - User exceeded agreed spending limits
  - Identity theft involving family member

## Customer Responsibilities

### During Investigation
- Respond promptly to information requests
- Provide truthful, accurate information
- Submit supporting documentation within 7 days
- Do not contact merchant after filing dispute
- Notify us immediately if issue is resolved with merchant

### Prohibited Actions
- Filing false or fraudulent disputes
- Disputing transactions made by authorized users
- Using disputes to avoid legitimate debts
- Failing to cooperate with investigation
- Destroying or withholding evidence

### Consequences of Misuse
- Dispute privileges may be restricted
- Account closure for repeated abuse
- Legal action for fraud
- Reported to credit bureaus
- Law enforcement referral if criminal activity suspected

## Outcomes

### Dispute Upheld (In Customer's Favor)
- Provisional credit becomes permanent
- Transaction removed from statement
- Associated fees refunded
- No impact on credit score
- May receive apology letter

### Dispute Denied (In Merchant's Favor)
- Provisional credit reversed
- Transaction remains on account
- Customer owes disputed amount
- Detailed explanation provided
- Right to appeal decision

### Partial Resolution
- Some but not all charges removed
- Compromise amount agreed upon
- Split liability between parties
- Customer accepts or can escalate
- Documented agreement required

## Appeals Process

### When to Appeal
- New evidence available
- Disagree with investigation findings
- Procedural error in original investigation
- Merchant evidence appears fraudulent

### How to Appeal
1. Submit appeal request within 30 days of decision
2. Provide new evidence or detailed reasoning
3. Appeal reviewed by senior disputes team
4. Decision within 15 business days
5. Final decision is binding

### Escalation Options
- File complaint with Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)
- Contact state banking regulator
- Seek legal counsel for large amounts
- Small claims court for unresolved issues
