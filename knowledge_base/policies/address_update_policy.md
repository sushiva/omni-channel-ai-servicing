# Address Update Policy

## Policy ID: POL-ADDR-001
## Version: 2024-01
## Intent: ADDRESS_UPDATE
## Last Updated: January 15, 2024

## Overview

Customers can update their mailing address through multiple channels including online banking, mobile app, phone banking, or in-branch visits. This policy outlines the verification requirements, processing timelines, and special considerations for address changes.

## Channels Available

### 1. Online Banking & Mobile App
- Self-service address update through secure portal
- Real-time validation of address format
- Instant confirmation upon submission
- Processing time: 1-2 business days

### 2. Phone Banking
- Call customer service at 1-800-555-BANK (2265)
- Available 24/7 with extended hours on weekends
- Verbal verification required
- Processing time: 3-5 business days

### 3. In-Branch
- Visit any branch location with valid ID
- Same-day processing available for simple changes
- Paper form completion required
- Processing time: Immediate to 1 business day

## Verification Requirements

### Standard Address Change (Same State)
Required documentation:
- Valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)
- Proof of new address dated within 60 days:
  - Utility bill (water, electric, gas, internet)
  - Lease agreement or mortgage statement
  - Bank statement from another institution
  - Government correspondence (DMV, IRS, etc.)

### Cross-State Address Change
Additional requirements:
- Secondary form of ID verification
- Both old and new address documentation
- Enhanced security review (may take additional 2-3 business days)
- Callback verification to previous phone number on file

### International Address Change
Special requirements:
- Embassy or consulate documentation may be needed
- Notarized proof of residence
- International mailing address must be verified through postal service
- Extended processing time: 5-10 business days
- Additional fees may apply for international mail delivery

## Processing Timeline

| Channel | Standard | Cross-State | International |
|---------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Online/Mobile | 1-2 business days | 3-5 business days | 7-10 business days |
| Phone | 3-5 business days | 5-7 business days | 10-15 business days |
| In-Branch | Immediate to 1 day | 2-3 business days | 5-10 business days |

## Special Cases

### PO Box Addresses
- **Accepted**: For mailing address only
- **Limitations**: 
  - Cannot be used as primary physical address
  - Cannot be used for card delivery (requires physical address)
  - Must maintain physical address on file for regulatory compliance
  - Some services may be restricted

### Military APO/FPO Addresses
- Accepted with valid military ID
- Standard domestic processing times apply
- Special handling for deployed personnel
- Temporary address option available (up to 12 months)

### Temporary Address Changes
- Available for 1-12 months
- Mail forwarding to temporary address
- Automatic reversion to permanent address after expiration
- Can be cancelled or extended before expiration
- Useful for: extended travel, seasonal residences, temporary work assignments

### Multiple Addresses on File
- Primary address: Used for all official correspondence
- Secondary address: Optional for convenience
- Can designate different addresses for:
  - Statement delivery
  - Card delivery
  - Tax documents
  - General correspondence

## Procedures

### Step 1: Initiation
Customer submits address update request via preferred channel with required documentation.

### Step 2: Validation
- System validates address format using USPS database
- Verifies address is deliverable
- Checks for fraudulent patterns
- Reviews customer's address change history

### Step 3: Verification
- Agent or automated system verifies supporting documentation
- Cross-references with existing account information
- Performs identity verification checks
- May require additional authentication for high-risk changes

### Step 4: Processing
- Address updated in core banking system
- Change propagated to all sub-systems:
  - Card services
  - Statement delivery
  - Digital banking profiles
  - Marketing preferences
  - Loan/mortgage servicing

### Step 5: Confirmation
- Confirmation sent to both old and new addresses (mail)
- Email and SMS notification sent immediately
- Mobile app notification
- Available for review in online banking transaction history

### Step 6: Monitoring
- 30-day monitoring period for unusual activity
- Alerts triggered for:
  - Card transactions at old address locations
  - Login attempts from old address region
  - Duplicate address update requests

## Impact on Services

### Immediate Impact
- Online banking: Address visible immediately after approval
- Mobile app: Updated address reflected in profile
- Customer service: New address available to agents

### Delayed Impact (24-72 hours)
- Credit/debit cards: New cards may be ordered to new address
- Check orders: Future orders ship to new address
- Statement delivery: Next statement cycle uses new address

### Manual Review Required
- Loan/mortgage servicing: May require separate notification
- Safe deposit box: Location-specific, requires in-branch update
- Wire transfer instructions: Security review before update

## Fraud Prevention

### Red Flags Monitored
- Multiple address changes within 90 days
- Address change followed immediately by large withdrawal
- Change to address associated with known fraud patterns
- Mismatch between customer's stated reason and documentation
- IP address location inconsistent with claimed new address

### Additional Verification Steps
- Enhanced authentication for high-risk changes
- Callback to phone number on file
- Email verification to confirmed email address
- Possible temporary hold on certain transactions (3-5 days)
- Manual review by fraud prevention team

### Customer Protection
- Notifications sent to all contact methods on file
- 48-hour grace period to dispute address change
- Easy reversal process if unauthorized
- Account monitoring for 30 days post-change

## Compliance Requirements

### Regulatory Framework
- **PATRIOT ACT**: Address verification required for AML compliance
- **FCRA**: Accurate address reporting to credit bureaus
- **GDPR**: Customer consent required for address data processing (for international customers)
- **State Regulations**: Some states require additional documentation or processing time

### Record Keeping
- All address changes logged with timestamp and user ID
- Supporting documentation retained for 7 years
- Audit trail maintained for regulatory review
- Change history available to customer upon request

### Privacy Considerations
- Address information classified as PII (Personally Identifiable Information)
- Access restricted to authorized personnel only
- Encryption required for address data in transit and at rest
- Address information not shared with third parties without consent

## Customer Communication

### Proactive Notifications
- 30 days before planned system maintenance affecting address updates
- Notice of policy changes affecting address verification requirements
- Reminder to update address when card expiration approaches

### Self-Service Resources
- Online FAQ covering common address change scenarios
- Video tutorials for online/mobile address updates
- Chat support available for real-time assistance
- Branch locator for in-person assistance

## Exceptions and Escalations

### When to Escalate
- Customer unable to provide required documentation
- Conflicting information in verification process
- Suspected fraud or identity theft
- International addresses in sanctioned countries
- Complex situations requiring management approval

### Escalation Process
1. Document the issue in case management system
2. Elevate to supervisor or specialist team
3. Contact customer within 24 hours with status update
4. Resolve within 3-5 business days or provide timeline
5. Follow up after resolution to ensure satisfaction
