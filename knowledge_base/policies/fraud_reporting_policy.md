# Fraud Reporting Policy

## Policy ID: POL-FRD-001
## Version: 2024-01
## Intent: FRAUD_REPORT
## Last Updated: January 8, 2024

## Overview

This policy outlines procedures for reporting, investigating, and preventing fraudulent activity on customer accounts. Early detection and prompt reporting are critical to minimizing losses and protecting customer accounts.

## Types of Fraud

### Account Takeover Fraud
- **Description**: Unauthorized access to customer's online banking
- **Indicators**:
  - Password/security question changes not initiated by customer
  - New devices or IP addresses accessing account
  - Email or phone number changes
  - Addition of unauthorized external accounts
  - Unusual transaction patterns

### Card Fraud
- **Credit/Debit Card Fraud**: Unauthorized use of physical or card details
- **Card-Not-Present Fraud**: Online purchases without physical card
- **Counterfeit Cards**: Cloned cards using stolen information
- **Lost/Stolen Cards**: Physical card in wrong hands

### Identity Theft
- **New Account Fraud**: Accounts opened using stolen identity
- **Synthetic Identity**: Combination of real and fake information
- **Account Information Misuse**: Personal details used for fraud

### Check Fraud
- **Forged Checks**: Fake signatures or altered checks
- **Check Washing**: Chemicals used to alter check details
- **Counterfeit Checks**: Completely fabricated checks
- **Remote Deposit Fraud**: Depositing checks multiple times

### Wire Transfer Fraud
- **Business Email Compromise (BEC)**: Fake vendor payment requests
- **Romance Scams**: Fraudulent relationship leading to money transfers
- **Emergency Scams**: Fake family emergency requiring immediate transfer
- **Investment Scams**: Fake investment opportunities

### ATM/Skimming Fraud
- **Card Skimmers**: Devices capturing card information at ATMs
- **PIN Cameras**: Hidden cameras recording PIN entry
- **Cash Trapping**: Devices preventing cash dispensing
- **Shimming**: Thin devices inside card readers

## Reporting Fraud Immediately

### How to Report

**24/7 Fraud Hotline:**
- **Toll-Free**: 1-800-555-FRAUD (37283)
- **International**: +1-555-555-5555 (collect calls accepted)
- **TTY**: 1-800-555-5555 for hearing impaired

**Online Banking/Mobile App:**
- Navigate to "Report Fraud" in security settings
- Select type of fraud from dropdown menu
- Provide details of suspicious activity
- Receive immediate case number
- Card automatically locked pending review

**In-Branch:**
- Visit any branch location immediately
- Bring valid ID and account information
- Complete fraud report with banker
- Immediate account protection measures initiated

### Information to Provide
- Account number(s) affected
- Date and time you noticed the fraud
- Specific transactions or activities in question
- How you discovered the fraud
- Any communication with suspected fraudster
- Whether you've filed a police report

## Immediate Actions We Take

### Account Protection (Within 15 Minutes)
- Deactivate compromised cards immediately
- Lock online banking access if compromised
- Place holds on pending suspicious transactions
- Block external account transfers
- Enable enhanced monitoring alerts

### Investigation Initiation (Within 1 Hour)
- Assign fraud case manager
- Review recent account activity (90 days)
- Check for related accounts at risk
- Contact customer for additional details
- Coordinate with fraud prevention team

### Provisional Credit (Within 10 Business Days)
- Credit account for unauthorized transactions
- Refund associated fees (overdraft, NSF, late fees)
- Restore funds to account balance
- Subject to final investigation outcome

## Investigation Process

### Phase 1: Initial Assessment (Day 1-2)
- Gather all relevant transaction details
- Interview customer about fraud discovery
- Analyze account access logs
- Review security question/password changes
- Check for similar fraud patterns

### Phase 2: Evidence Collection (Day 3-10)
- Request transaction documentation
- Contact merchants for purchase details
- Review IP addresses and device fingerprints
- Check surveillance footage (ATM/branch)
- Coordinate with card networks
- File law enforcement report if criminal activity

### Phase 3: Analysis (Day 11-30)
- Compare customer information with evidence
- Determine fraud type and entry point
- Assess customer's security practices
- Identify liability under regulations
- Consult with legal/compliance teams

### Phase 4: Resolution (Day 31-45)
- Make final determination on claims
- Issue permanent credits or reversals
- Close or reopen accounts as needed
- Provide detailed outcome letter
- Document lessons learned

## Customer Responsibilities

### Before Fraud Occurs (Prevention)
- Protect PINs and passwords (never share)
- Review account statements monthly
- Enable transaction alerts
- Use secure networks for online banking
- Keep contact information updated
- Shred sensitive documents
- Monitor credit reports regularly

### During Investigation
- Respond promptly to information requests
- File police report for criminal fraud
- Provide truthful and complete information
- Change passwords and security questions
- Review all accounts for suspicious activity
- Do not contact suspected fraudster

### After Resolution
- Monitor accounts closely for 6 months
- Review credit reports quarterly
- Consider identity theft protection services
- Update security practices
- Report any related issues immediately

## Fraud Prevention Measures

### Account Security Features

**Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):**
- Required for online banking login
- SMS code or authenticator app
- Biometric options (fingerprint, face ID)
- Mandatory for high-risk transactions

**Transaction Alerts:**
- Real-time SMS/email for transactions over threshold
- Daily balance notifications
- Foreign transaction alerts
- Card-not-present transaction alerts
- Failed login attempt notifications

**Spending Controls:**
- Daily transaction limits
- Per-transaction amount limits
- Geographic restrictions
- Merchant category blocking
- ATM withdrawal limits

### Monitoring Systems

**Real-Time Fraud Detection:**
- AI/ML models analyzing transactions
- Behavioral pattern analysis
- Velocity checks (multiple transactions in short time)
- Geo-velocity (transactions in different locations too quickly)
- High-risk merchant category flags

**Proactive Outreach:**
- Automated calls/texts for suspicious activity
- Must confirm or deny transaction
- Temporary card hold pending verification
- Re-enable after customer confirmation

**Address Change Monitoring:**
- 30-day enhanced monitoring after address change
- Alerts for card usage at old address
- Delayed large transactions
- Additional verification for card reissues

## Special Fraud Scenarios

### Elder Fraud
- Heightened protections for customers 65+
- Required callback for unusual wire transfers
- Family member alerts (with customer consent)
- Extended dispute timelines
- Referrals to Adult Protective Services

### Business Account Fraud
- Dual authorization requirements
- ACH positive pay services
- Wire transfer callbacks
- Employee training resources
- Segregation of duties recommendations

### Phishing/Vishing Attempts
- **We Will Never**:
  - Call asking for full account number
  - Request PIN or full password
  - Email links to login pages
  - Ask for security questions answers
  - Threaten account closure without written notice

### Authorized Push Payment (APP) Fraud
- Customer willingly sends money to fraudster
- Romance scams, investment fraud, impersonation
- More difficult to recover funds
- Prevention through education
- May report to receiving bank

## Liability and Customer Protection

### Zero Liability Policy
**Conditions for $0 Liability:**
- Fraud reported within 60 days
- Customer did not contribute to loss
- Reasonable security practices maintained
- Full cooperation with investigation

**When Liability May Apply:**
- Shared PIN/password with third party
- Delayed reporting beyond 60 days
- Gross negligence (leaving card with stranger)
- Participation in fraud scheme

### Regulatory Protections
- **Reg E (Debit)**: $50 max if reported within 2 days
- **Reg Z (Credit)**: $50 max for unauthorized charges
- **Bank Policy**: Usually $0 with prompt reporting

## Fraud Resolution Outcomes

### Fraud Confirmed
- Permanent credit issued
- Account fully restored
- Fees refunded
- New card/account numbers issued
- Enhanced monitoring for 6 months
- Fraud prevention tips provided

### Fraud Not Confirmed
- Provisional credit reversed
- Customer responsible for charges
- Detailed explanation provided
- Appeal rights explained
- May suggest police report if customer disagrees

### Customer-Authorized Transaction
- Customer gave permission (even if tricked)
- Technically not fraud under regulations
- May pursue as dispute instead
- Recovery depends on merchant cooperation
- Education on recognizing scams

## Recovery and Restitution

### Fund Recovery Efforts
- Work with receiving banks to freeze funds
- Coordinate with law enforcement
- Card network chargeback processes
- Civil litigation for large losses
- Asset forfeiture proceedings

### Additional Support
- Free credit monitoring (12 months)
- Identity theft restoration services
- New account numbers issued free
- Rush delivery of replacement cards
- Waived account fees during recovery

## Data Breach Response

### If Our Bank is Breached
- Immediate notification to affected customers
- Free credit monitoring and identity protection
- Replacement cards issued proactively
- Enhanced monitoring on all accounts
- Regular updates on incident investigation

### If Third-Party Breach Affects You
- Monitor for breach notifications
- Change passwords immediately
- Enable extra security features
- Review accounts for unusual activity
- Consider placing credit freeze

## Law Enforcement Cooperation

### When We File Reports
- Fraud losses over $5,000
- Organized fraud rings suspected
- Multiple accounts affected
- Identity theft rings
- Interstate or international fraud

### Customer Obligations
- File police report for identity theft
- Obtain case number for records
- Provide copy of report to us
- Cooperate with law enforcement
- May testify if case goes to trial

### Information Sharing
- Share data with law enforcement
- Report to Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
- Notify credit bureaus for identity theft
- Share patterns with industry groups (anonymized)
- Comply with subpoenas and legal requests
