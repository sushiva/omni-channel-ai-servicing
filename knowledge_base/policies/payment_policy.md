## Payment Types and Procedures

### Credit Card Payments

**Minimum Payment:**
- Calculated as greater of:
  - 2% of balance
  - $25
  - Full balance if under $25
- Due date: 25 days after statement closing

**Payment Options:**
- **Pay Minimum**: Lowest required payment
- **Pay Statement Balance**: Avoid interest charges
- **Pay Current Balance**: Include new transactions
- **Custom Amount**: Any amount â‰¥ minimum payment

**Payment Application Order:**
1. Previous minimum payment (if past due)
2. Fees and interest charges
3. Cash advances (highest APR)
4. Balance transfers
5. Purchases (by APR, highest first)

**Grace Period:**
- 25 days from statement closing date
- No interest if statement balance paid in full
- Lost if any balance carried over

### Loan Payments

**Regular Payments:**
- Due date: Same day each month
- Auto-pay available (no fee)
- Extra payments applied to principal
- Bi-weekly payment option available

**Payment Application:**
1. Late fees and charges
2. Accrued interest
3. Principal balance
4. Escrow (if applicable for mortgages)

**Principal-Only Payments:**
- Must specify "principal only"
- Reduces overall interest paid
- Shortens loan term
- No prepayment penalties

**Payment Holidays:**
- Available for good standing accounts
- Skip one payment per year
- Interest continues to accrue
- Must request 10 days before due date

### Bill Pay Service

**Supported Payees:**
- Utilities (electric, gas, water, internet)
- Credit cards (any bank)
- Loans and mortgages
- Rent payments (to landlord or property management)
- Government agencies (taxes, fees)
- Any business or individual with US address

**eBill Enrollment:**
- Electronic bill delivery
- Automatic payment amount capture
- Payment reminders
- Paperless and eco-friendly

**Payment Methods:**
- **Electronic**: Fast, secure, no check needed (2-3 days)
- **Check**: Mailed to payee (5-7 days)
- **Overnight Check**: For urgent payments (additional fee)

**Recurring Payments:**
- Fixed amount: Same amount each period
- Variable amount: Amount changes (eBill required)
- Frequency: Weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, semi-annual, annual
- Auto-pay: Set and forget convenience

### Person-to-Person (P2P) Payments

**Zelle:**
- Instant transfers (typically within minutes)
- Send to mobile number or email
- Recipient must enroll with Zelle
- Daily limit: $2,500
- No fees for personal accounts

**External P2P Services:**
- Venmo, PayPal, Cash App not directly integrated
- Can fund via debit card or bank transfer
- Subject to third-party terms and fees

## Payment Issues and Troubleshooting

### Common Payment Problems

**Payment Not Received/Posted:**
- **Check Processing Time**: Verify expected posting date
- **Weekend/Holiday Delays**: Add business days
- **Incorrect Account Number**: Contact us immediately
- **Insufficient Funds**: Payment may be returned
- **Technical Glitch**: Rare, but we investigate all reports

**Returned Payment:**
- **NSF (Non-Sufficient Funds)**: Account balance too low
- **Stop Payment**: Customer stopped the check
- **Account Closed**: Source account no longer active
- **Invalid Account**: Account number incorrect
- **Fee**: $35 returned payment fee (may be waived first time)

**Duplicate Payment:**
- Contact us within 60 days of statement date
- Provide payment confirmation numbers
- Investigation within 10 business days
- Refund processed if confirmed duplicate
- May require waiting one billing cycle

**Wrong Amount:**
- Review payment confirmation immediately
- Contact us within 10 days for correction
- Adjustment processed within 5 business days
- May show as reversal and new payment
- No fees for our errors

**Payment to Wrong Account:**
- Report immediately (within 24 hours best)
- We attempt to recall payment
- Success depends on payment type and timing
- May require contacting recipient
- Not always recoverable if already processed

### Expediting Payments

**When You Need Faster Processing:**
- **Wire Transfer**: Same day, $25 fee
- **Overnight Bill Pay Check**: Next day delivery, $15 fee
- **In-Branch Payment**: Same day posting
- **Early Online Payment**: Submit 3-5 days before due date

**Emergency Payment Situations:**
- Late payment fees approaching
- Risk of service disconnection
- Mortgage payment due date critical
- Avoiding loan default

We'll work with you to find fastest option available.

## Late Payments and Fees

### Late Payment Consequences

**Credit Card:**
- Late fee: $30 first time, $40 subsequent (within 6 months)
- Penalty APR may apply (up to 29.99%)
- Reported to credit bureaus if 30+ days late
- May lose promotional rates

**Loan:**
- Late fee: 5% of payment or $15, whichever is greater
- Reported to credit bureaus if 30+ days late
- Risk of acceleration (entire balance due)
- May affect future loan applications

**Prevention:**
- Set up automatic payments
- Enable payment reminders
- Allow 5-7 days for mailing payments
- Use online/mobile for faster processing

### Grace Periods and Forgiveness

**Late Fee Waiver:**
- First late payment may be forgiven
- Call customer service to request waiver
- Good payment history increases chances
- Extenuating circumstances considered (medical, disaster)

**Payment Extension:**
- Available for customers in good standing
- Extends due date by 5-10 days
- Request before current due date
- May not avoid credit reporting if already late

## Payment Security

### Fraud Prevention

**We Monitor For:**
- Payment amount anomalies
- New payee setup followed by large payment
- Rapid succession of payments
- Geo-location inconsistencies
- Payment to high-risk merchants or countries

**You Can Protect Yourself:**
- Never share online banking credentials
- Use secure networks for payments
- Verify payee information before submitting
- Keep confirmation numbers for your records
- Review account statements for unauthorized payments

**If You Suspect Fraud:**
- Call fraud hotline immediately: 1-800-555-FRAUD (37283)
- Change passwords and security questions
- Review all recent payments
- File police report if identity theft suspected
- Request payment stop if not yet processed

### Secure Payment Methods

**Encryption:**
- All online payments use 256-bit SSL encryption
- Mobile app payments equally secure
- Data encrypted in transit and at rest

**Authentication:**
- Multi-factor authentication required
- Biometric options available
- Session timeout for inactive periods
- Device registration for new logins

## Automatic Payments (Auto-Pay)

### Setting Up Auto-Pay

**Eligible Accounts:**
- Credit cards: minimum or statement balance
- Loans: regular payment amount
- Utilities and recurring bills
- Subscription services

**Scheduling Options:**
- Pay on due date
- Pay fixed number of days before due date
- Pay when balance reaches threshold

**Safety Features:**
- Maximum payment amount cap
- Low balance alerts
- Insufficient funds notification
- Confirmation email after each auto-payment

### Managing Auto-Pay

**Stopping Auto-Pay:**
- Must cancel at least 3 business days before scheduled payment
- Online banking: Immediate cancellation available
- Call customer service for same-day cancellation
- Confirm cancellation in writing for records

**Modifying Auto-Pay:**
- Change amount: Must do before scheduled date
- Change account: 5 business days notice required
- Change date: Subject to billing cycle limitations

## Special Payment Situations

### Large Payments (Over $10,000)

**Additional Verification:**
- Source of funds documentation may be required
- Enhanced fraud review (24-48 hours)
- May require callback confirmation
- Money laundering compliance checks

**Wire Transfer:**
- Recommended for large amounts
- Same-day processing guaranteed
- $25-$50 fee depending on amount
- Confirmation provided immediately

### International Payments

**Wire Transfer:**
- 1-3 business day processing
- SWIFT code and IBAN required
- Currency conversion fees apply
- Intermediary bank fees possible

**Currency Considerations:**
- Exchange rate applied at time of processing
- May differ from rate when initiated
- Foreign transaction fees: 3% of amount
- Consider locking in rate for large transfers

### Tax Payments

**IRS Payments:**
- Bill pay available to IRS
- Electronic payment options through IRS.gov
- Estimated quarterly tax payments supported
- Year-end payment deadline reminders

**State and Local Taxes:**
- Bill pay to state revenue departments
- Property tax payments to local governments
- Verify payee information carefully
- Keep receipts for tax records
